Jaggo Pre-Wedding Celebration

A "Jaggo" in a Punjabi wedding is a pre-wedding ceremony that translates to "wake up" where the bride and groom's families, particularly the maternal side, gather to sing, dance, and celebrate the upcoming wedding with a lively atmosphere. It often includes women carrying decorated pots with candles on their heads while dancing throughout the night.

Sikh Wedding (also known as Anand Karaj)

Beginning with Ardas, which is a set of prayers offered by the couple and their parents to "Waheguru"

Followed by a Milni, which is a ritual where members of both families greet each other individually

The main ceremony, Anand Karaj, which translates to "Blissful Union". The couple, their families, and the congregation sit in front of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism. The ceremony includes the couple walking around the Guru Granth Sahib four times while the priest recites the laavan, a marriage prayer.
